Your Secret Word — What Is a Password and Why Does It Matter?

A password is your secret word or phrase that protects your account. Think of it like the key to your house. If someone gets your key, they can walk in and take everything.

On your phone and on the internet, a weak password is like leaving your front door wide open. Criminals look for easy targets — and a simple password is exactly that.

A weak password leaves you wide open. A strong password locks criminals out — permanently.

What Makes a Strong Password?

  • Use at least 8 characters — the longer the better
  • Mix numbers, letters, and symbols like @, #, or !
  • Never use your name, date of birth, or phone number
  • Never use obvious patterns like "123456" or "password"
  • Use a different password for each account
⚠️ IMPORTANT — Never Share Your Password With Anyone
  • Safaricom will NEVER call you and ask for your M-Pesa PIN
  • Your bank will NEVER send you a message asking for your password
  • Your family members should not know your M-Pesa PIN — even your children
  • If anyone asks for your PIN or password — it is a trick. End the call immediately

A Simple Way to Create a Strong Password

Think of a sentence that means something to you. For example: "I moved to Nakuru in 1985 with my family."

Now take the first letter of each word: ImtNi1985wmf

Add a symbol at the end: ImtNi1985wmf#

That is a strong password that is easy for you to remember but very hard for a criminal to guess.

Staying Safe on WhatsApp and Facebook

WhatsApp and Facebook are wonderful tools for staying connected with family and friends. But they also attract people who want to trick or harm you.

WhatsApp Safety Rules

  1. 1
    Never send your M-Pesa PIN, ID number, or bank details on WhatsApp — even to family members
  2. 2
    Do not open links (blue clickable text) sent by people you do not know
  3. 3
    If someone you know sends you a strange message asking for money — call them first to confirm it is really them
  4. 4
    Do not share your WhatsApp verification code (the 6-digit number WhatsApp sends you) with anyone
  5. 5
    Set up two-step verification — an extra security step — on your WhatsApp (instructions below)

How to Set Up Two-Step Verification on WhatsApp

Two-step verification is an extra lock on your WhatsApp. Even if someone gets your phone number, they cannot access your WhatsApp without a secret PIN that only you know.

  1. 1
    Open WhatsApp on your phone
  2. 2
    Tap the three dots (⋮) in the top right corner
  3. 3
    Tap "Settings"
  4. 4
    Tap "Account"
  5. 5
    Tap "Two-step verification"
  6. 6
    Tap "Enable"
  7. 7
    Enter a 6-digit PIN that you will remember
  8. 8
    Enter your email address to help recover your account
  9. 9
    Tap "Save" — you are now protected ✅
✅ Well Done!

Setting up two-step verification is one of the best things you can do to protect yourself on WhatsApp. It takes less than 5 minutes and works 24 hours a day.

Fake News — How to Tell Truth From Lies Online

Kenya has a serious problem with fake news — false stories shared on WhatsApp, Facebook, and other platforms. These stories can look very real and can cause fear and confusion.

🔍

Before You Share Anything Online
Ask These 5 Questions

Fake news spreads because people share without thinking first

1
Where did it come from? Is it from Nation, Standard, or KBC? Or just a WhatsApp forward with no source?
2
When was it written? Old news shared as new news is one of the most common tricks in Kenya.
3
Does it make you feel very angry or afraid? Fake news is designed to make you emotional so you share without thinking.
4
Can you find it on another trusted website? If only one source is reporting it — be very suspicious.
5
Does it say "share urgently"? Real news never demands you share immediately.

When in doubt — do not share. Pause. Verify. Protect your family from lies.

Email Safety — How to Spot a Dangerous Message

An email is a message sent through the internet — like a letter but instant. Criminals send fake emails that look like they come from real companies — banks, M-Pesa, government offices.

These fake emails are called phishing emails (tricks designed to steal your information by pretending to be someone you trust).

Warning Signs of a Fake Email

  • It creates urgency — "Your account will be closed in 24 hours!"
  • It asks for your PIN, password, or ID number
  • It has spelling mistakes or strange language
  • The email address looks almost right but not quite — like "safaricom@gmail.com" instead of the real Safaricom address
  • It has a link asking you to "verify" your account
  • It promises you money, prizes, or gifts
⚠️ Golden Rule for Emails
  • If an email asks you to click a link and enter your details — do not do it
  • Go directly to the website yourself by typing the address into your browser
  • When in doubt, call the company directly using a phone number from their official website

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Someone called me claiming to be from Safaricom and asked for my PIN. What should I do?
Hang up immediately. Safaricom will never call you to ask for your PIN. This is a scam. Call Safaricom directly on 100 to report the number that called you.
I received money on M-Pesa from a stranger and they are asking me to send it back. What do I do?
Do not send any money. This is a common trick called the "wrong number scam." The original transaction may be reversed and you will lose your own money. Call Safaricom on 100 to report and verify immediately.
My grandchild set up my WhatsApp. How do I know it is safe?
Ask your grandchild to show you how to turn on two-step verification. This adds an extra PIN to your WhatsApp that only you know. Also check that your profile is set to "My Contacts" only so strangers cannot see your information.
I accidentally clicked a link in a WhatsApp message. What should I do?
Do not enter any information on the page that opened. Close it immediately. Change your WhatsApp and M-Pesa PIN as a precaution. If you entered any details, call Safaricom on 100 straight away.
How do I know if a news story on WhatsApp is real or fake?
Check if the story appears on Nation, Standard, or KBC news websites. If you cannot find it there, it is likely fake. Remember — fake news always makes you feel very emotional or urgent. Take a breath before you share anything.
My phone was stolen. What do I do about my M-Pesa?
Call Safaricom on 100 immediately from any phone. They can block your M-Pesa and SIM card so no one can access your money. You will need your ID number to verify your identity.
