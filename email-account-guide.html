Understanding Your Inbox

Your Gmail inbox is where received emails appear. Think of it like your postal mailbox. Here is what the different sections mean:

  • Primary (Inbox): This is where important emails from people and organisations arrive. Check this regularly.
  • Promotions: Emails from shops, advertisements, and newsletters go here automatically. Not usually urgent.
  • Spam: Emails Gmail thinks are dangerous or unwanted. Be very careful before opening anything here.
  • Sent: Copies of all emails you have sent — useful to check what you sent and when.
  • Drafts: Emails you started writing but did not send yet.
  • Trash: Deleted emails. Gmail keeps them for 30 days before permanently deleting.
Reading an Email
  • Unread emails appear in bold text — these are the new ones you haven't read yet
  • Tap on any email to open and read it
  • The sender's name or email address is shown on the left
  • The subject (topic) of the email is in the middle
  • The date it arrived is on the right

  • 2

  • 3

  • 4

Email Safety — Avoiding Scams

Email scams are very common in Kenya. Criminals send fake emails pretending to be from your bank, Safaricom, KRA, or even government offices. Here is how to protect yoursel[...]

⚠️ Never Do These Things
  • Never click links in emails from people or organisations you don't know
  • Never give your M-Pesa PIN, bank password, or ID number through email — no legitimate organisation will ever ask for this
  • Never open attachments (files) sent by strangers — they may contain viruses
  • If an email promises you have won money, it is almost certainly a scam — delete it immediately
  • Never share your Gmail password with anyone
How to Check if an Email is Real
  • Look at the sender's email address carefully — scammers often use addresses like safaricom-support@yahoo.com (real Safaricom emails use @safaricom.co.ke)
  • If unsure, call the organisation directly on their official phone number — don't use the number in the suspicious email
  • Genuine organisations never ask for passwords, PINs, or urgent payments by email

What Can You Do With Your Email?

Now that you have a Gmail account, a whole new digital world is open to you. Your single Gmail address gives you access to all of these:

  • eCitizen Kenya: Register at ecitizen.go.ke and access government services from your home
  • YouTube: Watch educational videos, Kenyan news, church sermons, and entertainment — all free
  • Google Maps: Find directions, locate hospitals, schools, and businesses near you
  • Google Drive: Store and share photos, documents, and important files for free
  • Online banking: Receive KCB, Equity, or Co-op Bank statements directly to your email
  • WhatsApp Web: Use WhatsApp on a laptop or tablet using your Google account
  • Job portals: Help your children or grandchildren apply for jobs through sites like BrighterMonday Kenya
