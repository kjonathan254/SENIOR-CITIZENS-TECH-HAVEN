The Problem

The Room Is Empty.
But Not for Long.

There is no Senior Planet in Lagos. There is no Cyber-Seniors in Nairobi. There is no GetSetUp in Accra. While the Global North has built mature ecosystems serving millions of older adults, Africa has no comparable architecture.

This is not a market failure. It is a category absence. Kenya, despite being Africa's digital success story with 40 million M-Pesa customers and 84.8% financial inclusion, exhibits the same vacuum.

This whitepaper introduces a new framework for understanding Africa's digital exclusion — and presents the roadmap for building the answer from the ground up.

"Managed Dependency"

The condition of being statistically included in digital systems while practically dependent on younger relatives for navigation. When Kenya reports 84.8% financial inclusion, the figure includes Grace in Nakuru — who receives money via M-Pesa but cannot independently send money, check her balance, or reverse a transaction. She is counted. She is not empowered.

"Digital Autonomy"

The goal is not merely to increase statistical inclusion. It is to achieve a condition where older adults can independently navigate digital systems, make informed choices, and participate in the digital economy as active agents — not passive recipients.
