What is eCitizen? Why Does It Matter for You?

eCitizen is the Government of Kenya's official online platform. It is a website where you can apply for government documents and services — from your phone or computer — without leaving your home.

Think of it like a government office that is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and never has a queue.

You can find it at www.ecitizen.go.ke on any phone or computer with internet. It is free to create an account. You only pay when you apply for a specific service.

What You Need Before You Start

Before you create your eCitizen account, make sure you have these four things ready. Do not start without them.

  • Your National ID card — you will need your ID number exactly as it appears on the card
  • An email address — this is where eCitizen will send you important messages. If you do not have one, ask a family member to help you create a Gmail account first
  • A phone number — your Safaricom, Airtel, or other Kenyan number. eCitizen will send a one-time code (OTP) to verify it is really you
  • M-Pesa on your phone — for paying fees when you apply for services
💡 Good to Know Before You Start
  • Creating an account is completely free. You only pay when applying for a specific service
  • Registration takes less than 10 minutes
  • You only need to create an account once. After that, you use the same account for all services
  • If you need help, go to your nearest Huduma Centre — the staff will assist you for free

How to Create Your eCitizen Account — Step by Step

Follow these steps carefully. Take your time. There is no rush.

Step 1: Open the eCitizen Website

  1. 1
    Open the app you use to search the internet (Google Chrome, Firefox, or any browser) on your phone or computer
  2. 2
    Type this address exactly: www.ecitizen.go.ke and press Enter or Go
  3. 3
    You will see the official eCitizen Kenya homepage with the Kenyan flag colours
⚠️ Important — Make Sure You Are on the Right Website
  • The real eCitizen website is www.ecitizen.go.ke — note the ".go.ke" at the end
  • Fake websites may look similar but have different addresses
  • Never enter your ID number on a website that does not have ".go.ke" in the address

Step 2: Click "Create Account"

  1. 1
    On the homepage, look for a button that says "Create Account" or "Register"
  2. 2
    Click or tap it
  3. 3
    You will be asked what type of user you are. Select "Kenyan Citizen"

Step 3: Fill in Your Personal Details

  1. 1
    Enter your National ID number exactly as it appears on your card
  2. 2
    Enter your first name as it appears on your ID
  3. 3
    Enter your year of birth as shown on your ID
  4. 4
    Enter your email address
  5. 5
    Enter your phone number
  6. 6
    Double-check everything before continuing — details must match your ID exactly

Step 4: Create Your Password

  1. 1
    Choose a strong password (your secret word) — mix letters, numbers and symbols
  2. 2
    Type it again to confirm
  3. 3
    Write it down somewhere safe at home — do not share it with anyone

Step 5: Verify Your Account

  1. 1
    Check your email — eCitizen will send you a verification link. Click on that link to confirm your email address
  2. 2
    Check your phone — eCitizen will also send a 6-digit code (OTP) to your phone number. Type that code into the box on the website
  3. 3
    Your account is now active. You are ready to use eCitizen
✅ Congratulations!

You have created your eCitizen account. You will never need to create it again. From now on, you simply log in with your email and password whenever you need a government service.

How to Log In After You Have Registered

Once your account is created, here is how to log in every time you want to use eCitizen.

  1. 1
    Go to www.ecitizen.go.ke on your phone or computer
  2. 2
    Click "Log In" or "Sign In"
  3. 3
    Enter your email address or ID number
  4. 4
    Enter your password (your secret word)
  5. 5
    eCitizen may send a one-time code to your phone. Enter it when prompted
  6. 6
    You are now inside your eCitizen account. You will see a list of government services available to you
💡 Forgot Your Password?

Click "Forgot Password" on the login page. eCitizen will send a reset link to your email address. Follow the instructions in that email to create a new password. If you need help, call a family member or visit your nearest Huduma Centre.

The 8 Most Useful Services for Older Adults

Once you are logged in, here are the services most commonly needed by older Kenyans and their families — and exactly how to access each one.

🛂 1. Passport Application and Renewal

You can now apply for or renew your Kenyan ePassport entirely online. You no longer need to visit Nyayo House in Nairobi.

  1. 1
    Log in to eCitizen and search for "Department of Immigration Services"
  2. 2
    Select "ePassport Application"
  3. 3
    Fill in the online form with your details
  4. 4
    Upload a recent passport-size photo
  5. 5
    Pay the required fee using M-Pesa
  6. 6
    Book an appointment at your nearest immigration office for biometrics (fingerprints and photo)
  7. 7
    Collect your passport when it is ready — you will receive an SMS notification
ℹ️ Passport Fees and Timeline
  • Standard processing: 10 to 14 working days after biometrics
  • You will receive SMS updates throughout the process
  • Check current fees on the official eCitizen portal as these change periodically

📋 2. Birth Certificate

If you need a copy of your birth certificate or your child's birth certificate, you can apply through eCitizen without visiting a government office.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "Civil Registration Department"
  2. 2
    Select "Birth Certificate"
  3. 3
    Fill in the required details
  4. 4
    Pay the fee using M-Pesa
  5. 5
    Select where you want to collect the certificate — your nearest Huduma Centre

🏛️ 3. Certificate of Good Conduct (Police Clearance)

This certificate proves you do not have a criminal record. It is required for employment, travel, and many other purposes.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "Directorate of Criminal Investigations"
  2. 2
    Select "Good Conduct Certificate"
  3. 3
    Fill in your details and pay the fee
  4. 4
    Book an appointment for fingerprinting at your nearest DCI office — you must appear in person for fingerprints
  5. 5
    Your certificate will be ready within a few weeks. You will get an SMS when it is done

🚗 4. NTSA Driving Licence Renewal

You can renew your driving licence online through the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) without queuing at their offices.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "NTSA"
  2. 2
    Select "Driving Licence Renewal"
  3. 3
    Confirm your details
  4. 4
    Pay the renewal fee using M-Pesa
  5. 5
    Your renewed licence will be posted to your address or you can collect from a designated centre

💰 5. KRA PIN Registration

Your KRA PIN (Kenya Revenue Authority Personal Identification Number) is required for many transactions in Kenya — opening a bank account, buying property, and more.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "Kenya Revenue Authority"
  2. 2
    Select "KRA PIN Registration" or "PIN Certificate"
  3. 3
    Fill in your details
  4. 4
    Your PIN certificate can be downloaded immediately. KRA PIN registration is free

🏥 6. SHIF (Formerly NHIF) — Health Insurance

The Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF) is Kenya's national health insurance. You can check your contributions and access health-related services through eCitizen.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "Social Health Authority" or "SHIF"
  2. 2
    Select the service you need — check contributions, download membership card, or update details
  3. 3
    Follow the instructions on screen

👴 7. NSSF — Pension and Retirement

The National Social Security Fund (NSSF) manages retirement savings for Kenyan workers. Older adults can check their balance and access benefits through eCitizen.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "NSSF"
  2. 2
    Select "Member Services"
  3. 3
    Check your contributions, update your details, or initiate a benefits claim

🏡 8. Land Services

You can now search land records, check title deed details, and pay land rent online through Ardhisasa — accessed through eCitizen.

  1. 1
    Log in and search for "Ardhisasa" or "Ministry of Lands"
  2. 2
    Select the land service you need
  3. 3
    Enter the land reference number or title deed number
  4. 4
    Pay any required fees using M-Pesa
Huduma Bora centres across Kenya provide in-person help for eCitizen services. If you are struggling, walk in and ask for assistance.

How to Pay Using M-Pesa on eCitizen

All eCitizen payments go through M-Pesa. Here is the exact process. Follow it carefully — especially the Paybill number, which changed in 2026.

💳 M-Pesa Payment Guide for eCitizen

Follow these steps exactly every time you pay for a government service

1
Complete your service application on eCitizen. At the end, eCitizen will generate a unique reference number for your payment — something like KLZWBGWR. Write it down.
2
Open M-Pesa on your phone. Select "Lipa na M-Pesa" then select "Pay Bill".
3
Enter the Business Number: 222222 (this is the new 2026 Paybill number — the old 206206 no longer works).
4
Enter the Account Number: your unique reference number from eCitizen (for example: KLZWBGWR). Type it exactly as shown.
5
Enter the exact amount shown on your eCitizen invoice, plus a small convenience fee of KES 50.
6
Enter your M-Pesa PIN and press OK. You will receive an M-Pesa confirmation SMS. Keep it for your records.
7
Go back to eCitizen. Your payment will be confirmed automatically within a few minutes. Your application will then be processed.
eCitizen M-Pesa Paybill Number (2026)
222222
The old number 206206 is no longer valid. Use 222222 for all eCitizen payments.
⚠️ Very Important Payment Warning
  • Always use the reference number generated by eCitizen as your account number — not your ID number or phone number
  • The old Paybill number 206206 no longer works. Always use 222222
  • Never pay anyone who claims they can process eCitizen payments on your behalf — this is a scam
  • Keep your M-Pesa confirmation SMS as proof of payment

How to Track Your Application

After you apply and pay, you do not have to wonder what is happening. eCitizen will keep you informed.

  1. 1
    Log in to eCitizen at www.ecitizen.go.ke
  2. 2
    Look for "My Applications" or "Application History" on your dashboard
  3. 3
    Click on your application to see its current status
  4. 4
    eCitizen will also send you SMS and email updates whenever your application moves to the next stage
  5. 5
    When your document is ready, you will receive an SMS telling you where and when to collect it
💡 Processing Times to Expect
  • Passport: 10 to 14 working days after biometrics appointment
  • Certificate of Good Conduct: 2 to 4 weeks after fingerprinting
  • Birth Certificate: 5 to 10 working days
  • KRA PIN: Immediate — download right away
  • Driving Licence Renewal: 7 to 14 working days

Common Problems and How to Fix Them

eCitizen is not always perfect. Here are the most common problems older adults experience — and exactly what to do about each one.

The Problem What to Do
The website is not loading Check your internet connection. Try again using mobile data instead of WiFi. Try a different browser like Chrome or Firefox.
I forgot my password Click "Forgot Password" on the login page. eCitizen will send a reset link to your email. Check your email and follow the instructions.
The OTP code did not arrive on my phone Wait 2 minutes and try again. Make sure your phone has network signal. Check that you entered the correct phone number.
My payment went through but eCitizen did not confirm it Wait 15 minutes — payments sometimes take time to reflect. If it still does not show, call eCitizen on 020 2370230 with your M-Pesa confirmation SMS ready.
My details do not match what is on the system Visit your nearest Huduma Centre with your original ID card. The staff will help you update your details on the system.
I cannot upload my photo or documents Make sure the file size is not too large — compress photos before uploading. Use JPG or PDF format. Ask a family member to help if needed.
The website shows an error message I do not understand Take a screenshot of the error message. Then call eCitizen support on 020 2370230 or visit your nearest Huduma Centre and show them the screenshot.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a computer to use eCitizen or can I use my phone?
You can use eCitizen on any smartphone with internet access. Open your phone's browser (Google Chrome works best), type www.ecitizen.go.ke, and follow the steps in this article. A computer is not required.
I do not have an email address. Can I still use eCitizen?
You need an email address to create an eCitizen account. Ask a family member to help you create a free Gmail account at www.gmail.com. It takes less than 5 minutes. Once your Gmail is ready, you can register on eCitizen.
What is the correct Paybill number for eCitizen payments in 2026?
The correct Paybill number is 222222. The old number 206206 is no longer valid. Always use the reference number generated by eCitizen as your account number when paying.
Can my son or daughter apply on eCitizen on my behalf?
Yes. A family member can help you create your account and fill in the application using your details. However, some services like passport and Good Conduct Certificate still require you to appear in person for biometrics or fingerprinting.
How long does it take to receive my document after applying?
It depends on the service. A KRA PIN is instant. A passport takes 10 to 14 working days after your biometrics appointment. A Good Conduct Certificate takes 2 to 4 weeks. eCitizen will send you SMS updates throughout the process.
I am in a village with no internet. How can I access eCitizen?
You can visit your nearest Huduma Centre, cyber cafe, or ask a family member in town to help you apply. Some Huduma Centres also have computers available for public use. Alternatively, most smartphones can access eCitizen using mobile data.
