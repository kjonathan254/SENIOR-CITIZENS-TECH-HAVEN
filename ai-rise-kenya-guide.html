What is Artificial Intelligence — In Plain Language

Imagine you have an extremely well-read assistant who has studied millions of books, articles, and conversations. This assistant can answer nearly any question, help you write a letter, translate a document, explain a medical term, or help you plan a journey — all in fluent, clear language. That is what AI is in its most accessible form.

AI is computer software that has been trained on enormous amounts of human knowledge — books, scientific papers, websites, conversations. By studying patterns in all this information, it learns to answer questions, write text, recognise images, and solve problems in ways that feel remarkably like speaking to a knowledgeable human being.

A Simple Way to Think About AI

AI is like a very fast, very well-read library. You can ask it a question in plain language and it will find the best answer from everything it has studied. Unlike a search engine (which shows you a list of links to browse), AI gives you a direct, conversational answer — and you can ask follow-up questions, just like talking to a person.

AI You Already Use Without Knowing It

You may be surprised to learn that AI is already part of your digital life. Here are everyday AI tools that millions of Kenyans use without realising they are using Artificial Intelligence:

🔍

Google Search

When you search on Google, AI analyses your question and ranks millions of results to show you the most relevant ones first.

🗺️

Google Maps

AI analyses real-time traffic data to suggest the fastest route. It learns from millions of drivers to make better predictions.

💬

WhatsApp Typing Suggestions

The words suggested as you type on WhatsApp are generated by AI — it learns your writing patterns to help you type faster.

📸

Camera Smart Features

When your phone automatically recognises faces, adjusts lighting, or identifies objects in photos — that is AI working in real time.

How AI Can Help Kenyan Seniors Specifically

AI offers specific, practical benefits for senior citizens. Here are the most meaningful ways AI can improve daily life for older Kenyans:

  • Instant answers to technology questions: Instead of waiting for a busy family member to help you, ask an AI assistant like Elsah or ChatGPT how to do something on your phone. It will explain step by step in simple language.
  • Health information: Ask AI to explain what a medical term means, what a medication is used for, or what symptoms might indicate. Always confirm with your doctor, but AI can help you understand and prepare better questions.
  • Translation: AI can translate Swahili to English and vice versa instantly. Useful for official documents, websites, and correspondence.
  • Writing help: Need to write a formal letter to a government office, a complaint to a company, or an application for your grandchild? AI can help you draft professional letters correctly.
  • News summary: Ask AI to summarise the latest Kenyan news or explain a complex political or economic situation in simple terms.
  • Farming and agriculture advice: Ask AI about crop diseases, best planting times for your region, or how to improve your soil — it draws on agricultural knowledge to give practical advice.

What to Watch Out For — AI's Limitations

AI is a powerful tool, but it has important limitations that every user must understand:

⚠️ Important AI Limitations
  • AI can make mistakes — it sometimes gives confidently wrong answers. Always verify important information, especially medical, legal, or financial advice, with a qualified professional.
  • AI does not know what is happening right now — its knowledge has a cutoff date. For current news or prices, use a news website.
  • Never share your M-Pesa PIN, bank passwords, national ID number, or other sensitive personal information with any AI chatbot
  • AI cannot contact government offices or banks on your behalf — it can only give you information and advice
