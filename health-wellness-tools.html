Why Health Technology Matters for Older Adults

Healthcare in Kenya has real challenges for older adults. Hospitals can be far away, transport is expensive, and waiting rooms are exhausting. For those managing chronic conditions like hypertension, diabetes, or arthritis — frequent hospital visits are both costly and tiring.

Technology is changing this. You can now monitor your blood pressure at home and share the readings with your doctor digitally. You can book a doctor consultation over the phone without travelling. You can set an alarm to remind you to take your medication at exactly the right time every day.

💡 How to Prepare for a Telemedicine Appointment
  • Find a quiet room with good lighting before your appointment
  • Write down your symptoms, how long you have had them, and any medications you currently take
  • Have your SHIF membership number ready in case the doctor needs it

Medication Reminders and Management

Missing medication is one of the most common health risks for older adults managing chronic conditions. A single missed blood pressure tablet or diabetes medication can have serious consequences. Your phone is your most reliable alarm clock — and setting it up takes less than two minutes.

The Simplest Method — Phone Alarms

  1. 1
    Open the Clock app on your Android phone
  2. 2
    Tap the alarm icon at the bottom
  3. 3
    Tap the plus (+) button to add a new alarm
  4. 4
    Set the time your medication is due
  5. 5
    Tap "Label" and type the name of the medication — for example "Blood pressure tablet"
  6. 6
    Select which days it should repeat — tap every day if you take it daily
  7. 7
    Tap Save. Repeat for each medication you take.
✅ Verified Medication Apps on Google Play Store

Medisafe Medication Manager — Available free on Google Play. Tracks multiple medications, sends reminders at the right time, and lets family members receive notifications if you miss a dose. Widely used and actively maintained in 2026.

MyTherapy Medication Reminder — Available free on Google Play. Tracks medications and health measurements in one place. Generates a health journal you can share with your doctor.

Fitness Trackers Available in Kenya
A healthcare worker helps an older Kenyan adult understand her fitness tracker readings. Learning to use health technology with guidance builds confidence and independence.

A fitness tracker is a small device worn on your wrist — like a watch — that monitors your health automatically throughout the day. It counts your steps, monitors your heart rate, tracks your sleep, and alerts you if readings seem unusual.

💡 For First-Time Fitness Tracker Users

Start with the Xiaomi Smart Band 8. It is affordable, simple to use, and has a large clear screen that is easy to read. The Huami Zepp Life app that controls it is straightforward in English. Ask a family member to help you set it up during their next visit — it takes about 15 minutes to configure and connects to your phone via Bluetooth.

⚠️ Important Health Technology Warning
  • Fitness trackers and health apps provide general wellness information — they are not a substitute for professional medical advice from a qualified doctor
  • If your tracker shows an irregular heart rate reading, do not diagnose yourself — consult your doctor and show them the data
  • Always tell your doctor about all symptoms you experience regardless of what your health app reports
  • Blood pressure readings from wrist-based trackers are less accurate than a proper blood pressure cuff — use them for trends, not diagnosis

Accessing SHIF Digitally

SHIF — the Social Health Insurance Fund — replaced NHIF in 2024 as Kenya's national health insurance scheme. Every Kenyan enrolled in SHIF can now access their membership information and make contributions digitally through M-Pesa and the SHA portal.

How to Access SHIF Through M-Pesa

  1. 1
    Dial *334# on your Safaricom phone to open M-Pesa
  2. 2
    Select Lipa na M-Pesa then Pay Bill
  3. 3
    Enter Business Number 200222
  4. 4
    Enter your ID number as the account number
  5. 5
    Enter the contribution amount and your M-Pesa PIN
  6. 6
    You will receive an SMS confirmation of your SHIF contribution

How to Check Your SHIF Status Online

  1. 1
    Open your phone browser and go to sha.go.ke
  2. 2
    Click Member Portal
  3. 3
    Log in using your national ID number and the password you set at registration
  4. 4
    You can view your contribution history, benefit status, and download your membership certificate
ℹ️ SHIF Registration Help

If you are not yet registered with SHIF or need to update your details, visit your nearest Huduma Centre with your national ID card. SHIF registration is also available through eCitizen at ecitizen.go.ke. Staff at Huduma Centres can help you register and set up digital access at no charge.

Using Health Technology Safely
  • Download apps only from the official Google Play Store — never from links sent by strangers on WhatsApp or SMS
  • Do not share your medical information with unverified apps, unknown websites, or people who contact you claiming to be health professionals
  • Verify telemedicine providers are licensed before your first appointment — Daktari Online and Zuri Health are both verified and regulated in Kenya
  • Show your doctor your health app data at every appointment — they can explain what the numbers mean and adjust your treatment if needed
  • Ask a trusted family member to help you set up health apps for the first time — do not do it alone if you are unsure
  • Keep your SHIF membership number written down somewhere safe at home alongside your national ID number

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really see a real qualified doctor through my phone in Kenya?
Yes. Daktari Online and Zuri Health both operate with licensed Kenyan doctors who are registered with the Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council. A telemedicine consultation is a real doctor appointment — the doctor can diagnose common conditions, prescribe medication, and refer you to a specialist or hospital if needed. For serious emergencies, always go to a physical hospital.
Is my SHIF membership still valid after the change from NHIF?
If you were registered with NHIF, your membership was automatically migrated to SHIF. However, you should verify your status by visiting sha.go.ke or going to your nearest Huduma Centre with your national ID. Some members need to update their details or re-register dependants under the new system. Huduma Centre staff will help you at no charge.
I have never used a fitness tracker before. Is it difficult to set up?
The Xiaomi Smart Band 8 is the most straightforward for first-time users. The setup involves downloading the Zepp Life app from the Google Play Store, creating a simple account, and connecting the band to your phone via Bluetooth. Ask a family member to help you the first time — the setup takes about 15 to 20 minutes. After that, the tracker works automatically without you needing to do anything.
My tracker shows my heart rate is high. Should I go to hospital immediately?
A single high reading on a fitness tracker does not necessarily indicate a problem. Trackers can give inaccurate readings if the band is too loose, if you have just been active, or if you are stressed. Take the reading again after resting for 10 minutes. If it remains consistently high over several readings, call your doctor or use a telemedicine service like Daktari Online for advice before travelling to hospital. If you are experiencing chest pain, difficulty breathing, or feel unwell alongside the high reading, call 999 or 1199 immediately.
Are telemedicine consultations covered by SHIF?
SHIF coverage for telemedicine is still developing in Kenya as of 2026. Currently, most SHIF outpatient benefits apply at accredited physical facilities. M-TIBA by Safaricom works with some SHIF arrangements at partner hospitals. Check directly with your telemedicine provider and SHIF to confirm your specific coverage. Daktari Online and Zuri Health consultations are affordable even without insurance coverage at KES 300 to 500 per session.
My phone is an old Android — will these health apps work on it?
Most health apps listed in this article work on Android phones running version 8.0 (Oreo) or higher, which covers the majority of smartphones sold in Kenya from 2018 onwards. Google Fit, Blood Pressure Diary, Medisafe, and Calm all have low system requirements. To check your Android version, go to Settings, then About Phone, then Software Information. If your phone is very old and apps do not install, the phone alarm method for medication reminders works on every phone regardless of age.
