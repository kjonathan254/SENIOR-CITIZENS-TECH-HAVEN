Kenya has led the world in mobile money for over a decade. M-Pesa, Lipa na M-Pesa, M-Shwari, KCB M-Pesa, digital banking apps — these tools have transformed how Kenyans manage money. They save time, reduce travel, and put financial control directly in your hands.

For older adults, the benefits are especially significant. No more travelling to a bank branch. No more queuing at a pay point. Your money is accessible from anywhere in Kenya, at any hour, from the phone in your pocket.

This guide walks you through each financial technology tool available to you — in plain language, step by step.

Why Financial Technology Matters for Older Adults

Managing money used to mean physical trips — to the bank, to the pay point, to the post office. For older adults with mobility challenges, those living far from towns, or those who find long queues exhausting, this was a real burden.

Financial technology changes this completely. Today, an older adult in Homa Bay can pay their Kenya Power electricity bill, check their NSSF pension balance, send money to a grandchild in Nairobi, and save for an emergency — all from a basic smartphone, without leaving home.

🏠

No Travel Needed

Pay bills, send money, and check balances from home — no bank visits, no queues.

⏰

Available 24 Hours

M-Pesa and banking apps work at any hour of the day or night, every day of the year.

🔒

Safer Than Cash

Carrying less cash reduces the risk of theft. Your money stays safely in your M-Pesa account.

📊

Full Financial Record

Every M-Pesa transaction is recorded automatically — giving you a complete history of your money.

2
How to Use M-Pesa Safely

M-Pesa is Kenya's most widely used financial tool. It allows you to send money, receive money, pay for goods and services, pay bills, save, and access credit — all from your Safaricom phone.

You access M-Pesa by dialling *334# on your Safaricom phone, or through the M-Pesa app if your phone supports it.

An older Kenyan adult using M-Pesa at a market in Kenya. M-Pesa is now available at over 176,000 agent locations across the country.

💚 How to Send Money on M-Pesa

The most commonly used M-Pesa service — follow these steps exactly

1
Dial *334# on your Safaricom phone or open the M-Pesa app
2
Select "Send Money" from the menu
3
Enter the recipient's phone number carefully — double-check before continuing
4
Enter the amount you want to send in Kenyan Shillings
5
Enter your M-Pesa PIN — your secret 4-digit number
6
Press OK. You will receive an SMS confirmation. Keep it for your records.
⚠️ Always Check the Phone Number Before Sending
  • One wrong digit sends your money to a stranger. Always read the number back before pressing OK.
  • If you sent money to the wrong number, call Safaricom immediately on 100. Do it within minutes for the best chance of recovery.
  • Your M-Pesa PIN is your most important secret. Never share it with anyone under any circumstances.
3
Lipa na M-Pesa — Paying for Goods and Services

Lipa na M-Pesa allows you to pay for goods and services at shops, supermarkets, hospitals, schools, and markets directly from your M-Pesa — without using cash. You will see the green and white Lipa na M-Pesa sign at most business premises across Kenya.

There are two ways to pay using Lipa na M-Pesa: Pay Bill (for businesses with a Paybill number) and Buy Goods (for businesses with a Till number).

How to Pay Using Lipa na M-Pesa

  1. 1
    Dial *334# or open the M-Pesa app on your phone
  2. 2
    Select "Lipa na M-Pesa"
  3. 3
    Select "Pay Bill" for a Paybill number or "Buy Goods" for a Till number
  4. 4
    Enter the business number shown on the Lipa na M-Pesa sign at the business
  5. 5
    Enter the account number if required — this is usually your phone number or account reference
  6. 6
    Enter the exact amount to pay
  7. 7
    Enter your M-Pesa PIN and press OK
  8. 8
    You will receive a confirmation SMS. Show it to the cashier as proof of payment.
✅ Where You Can Pay with Lipa na M-Pesa

Naivas, Quickmart, Carrefour and most supermarkets. Hospitals and pharmacies. Schools and colleges. Government offices including Huduma Centres. Fuel stations. Restaurants and hotels. Most market stalls and small shops displaying the green M-Pesa sign.

💡 Save Your Paybill Numbers

Write down the Paybill numbers you use most often on a small piece of paper and keep it near your phone. This saves you having to remember them each time. Your electricity token number, water account number, and SHIF membership number are worth writing down too.

Useful USSD Codes Every Older Adult Should Know

USSD codes are short number sequences you dial on your phone like a regular call. They work without internet, without an app, and on even the most basic Safaricom phone. These are essential tools — especially when your internet is not working.

Dial This Code What It Does
*334# Opens the full M-Pesa menu — send money, pay bills, check balance, everything
*234# Change your M-Pesa PIN — use this immediately if you think someone knows your PIN
*234*1# Check your M-Pesa balance without opening the full menu
*234*0# Check your last 5 M-Pesa transactions — useful for verifying payments
*544# Access M-Shwari — check your savings balance and apply for a loan
*654# Access KCB M-Pesa savings and loan services
*254# Access Hustler Fund — Kenya Government digital savings and loan fund
100 Call Safaricom customer care — free, available 24 hours, for all M-Pesa issues
ℹ️ Important — No Internet Required

All USSD codes above work without internet or mobile data. They use Safaricom's network directly. This means they work even in areas with poor internet signal, when your data bundle is finished, or on a basic phone without smartphone features.

Mobile Banking Apps

Most major Kenyan banks now have smartphone apps that let you check your balance, transfer money, pay bills, and manage your account without visiting a branch. If you have a bank account, ask your bank to help you set up their mobile app during your next visit.

🏦

KCB App

Check your KCB balance, pay bills, transfer money, and apply for KCB M-Pesa loans. Free on Google Play.

🏦

Equity Mobile

Full Equity Bank services on your phone. Check balance, send money to M-Pesa, and manage your account anywhere.

🏦

Co-op MCo-opCash

Cooperative Bank mobile banking. Transfer money, pay bills, and check your account from your phone.

🏦

Absa Kenya App

Full Absa Mobile Banking for account holders. Transfers, bill payments, and account management.

💡 Setting Up Your Bank App Safely

Visit your bank branch in person to register for mobile banking. Bring your national ID and the phone you will use. Bank staff will set up the app securely and show you how to use it. Never set up mobile banking by following instructions sent by SMS or WhatsApp — always go to the branch in person.

7
Digital Savings Accounts

Digital savings accounts accessible through M-Pesa allow you to earn interest on your savings and access emergency credit when needed — without ever visiting a bank.

Product What It Does How to Access Important Note
M-Shwari Savings account with interest plus small emergency loans Dial *544# or M-Pesa menu → M-Shwari Minimum save any amount. Interest paid monthly.
KCB M-Pesa Savings plus loans from KES 500 to KES 1,000,000 Dial *654# or M-Pesa menu → KCB M-Pesa Loan amount depends on your M-Pesa usage history.
Hustler Fund Government-backed digital savings and loan fund Dial *254# on Safaricom 25% of every loan goes to your savings automatically.
Fuliza M-Pesa overdraft for when your balance runs short Automatically activated during M-Pesa transactions Use with caution — interest charges accumulate daily.
⚠️ A Serious Warning About Fuliza
  • Fuliza charges daily interest that accumulates quickly. A small Fuliza balance can grow significantly if not repaid promptly.
  • Many older adults have found themselves in unexpected debt from Fuliza. Only use it for genuine emergencies.
  • To check your Fuliza balance, dial *234# and select Fuliza. Repay it as soon as money enters your M-Pesa account.
  • You can opt out of Fuliza by calling Safaricom on 100 if you prefer not to have it active on your account.
8
Staying Safe at an M-Pesa Agent

M-Pesa agents are found across Kenya — in shops, kiosks, and market stalls displaying the green M-Pesa sign. They help you deposit cash into your M-Pesa, withdraw cash, and register new accounts. Here is how to use an agent safely.

  1. 1
    Only use official M-Pesa agents displaying the current green and white Safaricom M-Pesa signage. If a sign looks old, faded, or unofficial, find another agent.
  2. 2
    Never hand your phone to the agent. You should always enter your own PIN. A legitimate agent will never need to touch your phone to complete a transaction.
  3. 3
    Cover the screen with your hand or body when entering your PIN. Be aware of people standing behind or beside you.
  4. 4
    Count your cash carefully before leaving the agent. Once you walk away, it is difficult to dispute the amount.
  5. 5
    Wait for your confirmation SMS before leaving. If you do not receive one within two minutes, call Safaricom on 100 before leaving the agent's location.
  6. 6
    If an agent asks for your PIN or claims there is a "problem with the system" that requires your PIN — leave immediately and call Safaricom on 100.
✅ Your Rights as an M-Pesa Customer

You have the right to refuse any agent who asks for your PIN. You have the right to count your money before leaving. You have the right to call Safaricom's free helpline (100) at any time if something feels wrong. A legitimate agent will never make you feel rushed or pressured.

9
Protecting Yourself from Financial Scams

Financial scams targeting older Kenyans are increasing. Here is what you must know to protect your money.

⚠️ Golden Rules — Never Break These
  • NEVER share your M-Pesa PIN with anyone — not your children, not a Safaricom agent, not a bank officer. No legitimate person will ever ask for your PIN.
  • Never send money to someone claiming you have won a prize. This is always a trick.
  • Ignore messages saying your M-Pesa account has been suspended and you must call a number. Call Safaricom's official line on 100 or 0722 000100 only.
  • Never reverse a transaction for a stranger who "accidentally" sent you money. Call Safaricom first and let them handle it.
  • Do not click links in SMS messages claiming to be from your bank or Safaricom. Always go directly to the official app or call the official number.
  • Never give your bank card number, CVV number, or internet banking password to anyone over the phone or on WhatsApp.
💡 If You Think You Have Been Scammed
  • Call Safaricom immediately on 100 (free) — they may be able to reverse the transaction if you act within minutes
  • Call your bank's 24-hour helpline immediately to freeze your account if needed
  • Report to your nearest police station or call the DCI on 0800 722 203 (toll-free)
  • Tell a trusted family member immediately so they can help you take action quickly
  • Report to the Communications Authority of Kenya on 0800 722 227 (free)

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

I forgot my M-Pesa PIN. What do I do?
Dial *234# on your Safaricom phone and select "Change PIN." You will be asked to enter a new PIN of your choice. If you are unable to reset it yourself, visit your nearest Safaricom shop with your national ID card and they will reset it for you at no charge.
How do I check how much money is in my M-Pesa?
Dial *234*1# on your Safaricom phone. You will receive an SMS showing your M-Pesa balance. You can also dial *334#, select My Account, then Balance. Your balance is shown for free without any charge.
Someone sent money to my M-Pesa by mistake and is asking me to send it back. What do I do?
Do not send any money back directly. This is a common trick. The original transaction may be fake or reversed later, meaning you would lose your own money. Call Safaricom on 100 immediately, explain the situation, and let Safaricom handle the reversal through their official process. Never send money to a stranger for any reason.
Is it safe to use mobile banking apps on my phone?
Yes — when set up correctly and used carefully. Always download your bank's app only from the official Google Play Store. Never download banking apps from links sent by SMS or WhatsApp. Set a strong PIN or fingerprint lock on your phone. Never use banking apps on someone else's phone. If your phone is lost or stolen, call your bank immediately to freeze the app.
What is the difference between M-Shwari and KCB M-Pesa?
Both are digital savings and loan products accessible through M-Pesa. M-Shwari is operated by NCBA Bank through Safaricom. KCB M-Pesa is operated by KCB Bank through Safaricom. Both let you save money and earn interest. KCB M-Pesa tends to offer larger loan amounts for users with strong M-Pesa usage history. You can have both active at the same time.
My children want to help me manage my finances digitally but I am worried about giving them access to my accounts. Is this safe?
Your concern is valid. You should never share your M-Pesa PIN or banking passwords with anyone, including family members. Instead, ask your children to sit with you and guide you through transactions rather than doing them on your behalf. If you genuinely need someone to manage your finances, speak to your bank about formal arrangements like a mandate or power of attorney — these are safer and more accountable than sharing passwords.
